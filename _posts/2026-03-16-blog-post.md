# How to be comfortable with your presentation

## Read the slides!

Reading the slides out loud helps a lot - try to go somewhere quiet, I get embarassed by this if someone is listening.

## If possible, practice presenting to a friend

Once you've got some slides, try actually presenting them to someone. This does a couple of things:

1. It lets them ask questions, or point out things they didn't get
2. It gets you an idea of how long the presentation will _really_ take

## The two most common problems

(I do both of these! Still, after lots of years of doing this)

* Trying to put too many words on a slide. I always want to do this, but remember people can get distracted reading instead of listening to you, and also too many words means the text has to _get smaller_ and harder to read :)
* Talking too fast. I do this when I get enthusiastic! The best way round this is to remember to **breathe**. Follow the punctuation as well.

## Getting comfortabl on the day

I like to move a chair, or something.

And it really helps to plug in your laptop early in the day an convince yourself the slides do come up on the screen!
